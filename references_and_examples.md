### Real-World Examples

#### Digital Management Approaches
1. **Spotify's Squad Model (Systems Approach)**
   - Autonomous, cross-functional teams
   - Interconnected through shared goals and communication protocols
   - Continuous feedback and improvement loops

2. **Netflix's Adaptive Culture (Contingency Approach)**
   - High-performance culture with situational adaptation
   - Data-driven decisions about organizational structure
   - Flexibility based on market conditions and performance metrics

3. **Google's People Operations (Behavioral Approach)**
   - People-centered practices using data analytics
   - Focus on psychological safety and team effectiveness
   - Experimentation with management practices

4. **Amazon's Decision-Making Framework (Decision-Theory Approach)**
   - Data-driven decision making
   - Two-way door vs. one-way door decisions
   - Metrics-based evaluation of choices

#### Digital Leadership Styles
1. **Satya Nadella at Microsoft (Transformational)**
   - Shifted culture from "know-it-all" to "learn-it-all"
   - Emphasized empathy and growth mindset
   - Led cloud transformation and cultural change

2. **Reed Hastings at Netflix (Participative)**
   - Data-driven participative decision making
   - High context, not high control approach
   - Freedom and responsibility culture

3. **Elon Musk at Tesla/SpaceX (Visionary)**
   - Clear long-term vision (sustainable transport, Mars colonization)
   - Innovation-focused leadership
   - Future-oriented strategy

#### Digital Leadership Capabilities
1. **Digital Champions:**
   - Salesforce's Ohana Culture Advocates

   - Internal influencers promoting technology adoption

2. **Digital Pioneers:**
   - Google's X Lab Leaders

3. **Digital Transformers:**

#### Motivational Strategies in Digital Companies
1. **Google's Approach (Maslow + Herzberg + Vroom)**
   - Self-actualization: 20% time for personal projects
   - Hygiene factors: Excellent technical infrastructure, flexible work
   - Motivators: Challenging projects, recognition systems
   - Expectancy: Clear performance-reward linkage through OKRs

2. **GitHub's Approach (Participative + Autonomy)**
   - Participative culture with distributed decision-making
   - Autonomy in work style and schedule
   - Open source principles applied internally

3. **Atlassian's Approach (Recognition + Growth)**
   - Internal recognition systems (Kudos)
   - Professional development opportunities
   - Hackathons and innovation time

### Frameworks and Models

#### Gibbs' Reflective Cycle
1. Description: What happened?
2. Feelings: What were your reactions?
3. Evaluation: What was good and bad?
4. Analysis: How else could you interpret the situation?
5. Conclusion: What else could you have done?
6. Action Plan: If it happens again, what will you do?
